Section 01 — Gaussian Coin Flip Board
Intuition
Each flip is ±1 (heads or tails). Run thousands of independent trials and watch the Central Limit Theorem emerge — the sum of random variables converges to a bell curve regardless of the underlying distribution. This is the foundation of all Monte Carlo reasoning.

Mean = n×(2p−1)  |  σ = 2√(np(1−p))  |  As n→∞ the histogram converges to N(mean, σ²) regardless of p. This is why miners can model revenue as a Gaussian even though each block find is a Bernoulli trial.

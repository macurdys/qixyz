Onboarding Scripts

What Do I Actually Say?

Most people don't know how to start the conversation. Here are tested scripts for different situations. Pick the one that fits, adapt it, make it yours.

The 30-Second Pitch Friends, coworkers, anyone curious
For When Someone Asks "What Are You Into Lately?"
YOU
"I've been working on something called Qi — it's a new kind of digital money that's actually backed by energy. Like, real electricity goes in, and the money comes out. It's not like Bitcoin where the energy is wasted — the energy is the value."
THEM
"Wait, how does that work?"
YOU
"Think of it like this — a dollar used to be backed by gold. Qi is backed by kilowatt-hours. Miners convert electricity into Qi tokens, and the cost of that energy becomes the floor price. It can't go to zero because there's real energy behind every token."
Tip: Don't oversell. Let them be curious. If they lean in, offer to show them the wallet. If they don't, drop it — you planted the seed.
The Merchant Pitch Coffee shops, farmers markets, freelancers
For Buying Something With Qi For the First Time
YOU
"Hey, I know this is a little different — but I'm part of a community testing a new digital currency called Qi. Would you be open to accepting it for [this coffee / this art / this service]? I'll pay the full price, and I can walk you through how it works in about two minutes."
THEM
"I don't know, I've never done crypto before..."
YOU
"Totally fair. Here's the deal — I'll also pay you in cash for the [item] right now so there's zero risk to you. But I'll send you the Qi too, as a bonus. That way you get to see how it works with nothing on the line. If you like it, you've got money in a new wallet. If not, no worries."
Tip: The dual-payment approach removes all friction. They get their cash and free Qi. Once it's in their wallet and they see the balance, curiosity takes over. This is the most effective grassroots onboarding method.
The DM / Online Twitter, Discord, freelance platforms
For Reaching Out to Someone Online
YOU
"Hey [name] — I've been following your [work/art/content] and I'd love to commission something from you. Quick question: would you be open to receiving payment in a new cryptocurrency called Qi? It runs on the Quai Network and is energy-backed. I'm happy to walk you through setup — it takes about 3 minutes."
Tip: Lead with the commission, not the crypto. They hear "I want to pay you" before they hear "in crypto." If they hesitate, offer to pay in their preferred method + send Qi as a bonus.
Objection Handling When they push back
Common Pushback and How to Respond
THEM
"Isn't crypto just a scam?"
YOU
"A lot of it is, honestly. That's why Qi is different — it's not built on hype. It's built on energy. Each token costs real electricity to produce, so it has a measurable cost floor. It can't be printed out of thin air."
THEM
"I don't understand blockchain stuff."
YOU
"You don't need to. Do you understand how Venmo works on the inside? Same idea. I'll set up your wallet — it takes 2 minutes — and I'll send you some Qi. You'll see a balance show up, just like any payment app."
THEM
"Can I actually spend it on anything?"
YOU
"That's literally what we're building right now. Every person who accepts Qi makes the network more useful. You'd be one of the first — that's a cool position to be in."
Tip: Never argue. Validate their concern, then redirect. The Venmo comparison is your best friend — everyone understands "payment app with a balance."
